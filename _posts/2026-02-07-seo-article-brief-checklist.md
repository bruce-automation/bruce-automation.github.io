A good brief is not “a long doc”. It’s the **right inputs** to write fast and match search intent.

## 1) Intent (one sentence)
- Informational: “how to…”, “guide…”, “examples…”
- Commercial: “best…”, “vs…”, “alternatives…”
- Transactional: “template…”, “tool…”

> One URL = one primary intent.

## 2) Primary keyword + 5 secondary keywords
- 1 primary keyword
- 3–8 secondary keywords (variants / sub-intents)

## 3) One-sentence promise
What the reader can do after reading.

## 4) Outline (H2/H3)
- 5–9 H2 max
- each H2 answers a real sub-intent
- add a FAQ section only when useful

## 5) Sources (anti-BS)
- official docs
- pricing pages
- reputable technical references

Rule: if you can’t source it, write it as **experience/opinion**.

## 6) Originality angle
Why this article deserves to exist:
- a downloadable checklist
- concrete examples
- a small dataset / mini-study
- a reproducible test

## 7) Internal linking
Before writing:
- 1 hub page
- 2–5 sibling articles
- 1 conversion page (RSS/newsletter/tools)

## 8) One CTA
Examples:
- subscribe via RSS
- join the newsletter
- read the hub
